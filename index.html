My story

Why structure matters to me.

I did not enter financial advisory because I loved selling products. I entered for income scalability. The reason I stayed is very different.

My first real lesson about money came from a mistake. In my early twenties, I started an overseas F&B franchise with two close friends. Within three months, serious cashflow issues exposed the gaps in our planning. We had not properly forecasted expenses, calculated runway, or stress-tested worst-case scenarios.

That experience taught me that financial problems are rarely about intelligence. They are usually about structure, clarity, and emotional decisions made under pressure. Over the past six years, I have carried that lesson into every client plan: use a factual approach to calculate risk and a behavioural approach to manage decisions.

“The best plan is not the one that looks best on paper. It is the one that can survive real life.”
